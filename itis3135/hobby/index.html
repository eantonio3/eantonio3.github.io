Home

Welcome to my hobby website! My hobby is collecting NASCAR memorabilia. From vintage hats and t-shirts to 1:64 scale diecasts, and even a barbie doll, I collect all sorts of NASCAR related stuff.

About

NASCAR, which stands for the National Association for Stock Car Auto Racing, is the most popular motorsport series in the United States. It is known for its competitive racing featuring stock cars that are modified for racing. The motorsport has a large and dedicated fan base, known for its passionate support of drivers and teams. Those teams and drivers are funded by corporate sponsors which allow for unique paint schemes and merchandise.

I am a passionate NASCAR fan, dedicated to building a cool collection of memorabilia, die-casts, and more. My love for NASCAR stems from family traditions and overall the love of cars turning very fast and very left.

Season

The NASCAR season lasts almost all year long with a short off-season in the winter months. From early February to November there is always NASCAR racing every weekend. The best time to get new NASCAR memorabilia is when visiting a racetrack. Thrift shopping and online auctions are great places to get used items.

The best time to get new NASCAR memorabilia for cheap is during the end of the season and during the off-season. This is because most of the merchandise gets put on clearance since new merchandise will be made for the next season.

Best Locations

Charlotte, North Carolina is an extremely important city to NASCAR due to most of the team race shops being located in the area. It is also home to the Charlotte Motor Speedway in Concord and the NASCAR Hall of Fame in Uptown.

The best locations to find vintage NASCAR memorabilia are thrift stores and flea markets in the Charlotte area. Since NASCAR peaked in the 90s and 2000s, most of the merchandise from that era has ended up at these places for cheap prices. Another place to find NASCAR memorabilia is on online marketplaces such as eBay, Poshmark, and Facebook Marketplace.

How

The first step to starting a collection would be to gain interest in the motorsport. Watching the races and understanding that it's much more than just "fast cars going left" is a good start.

The next step is deciding what type of items you want to collect, such as wearable memorabilia, die-casts, or race programs. Then, hit up thrift stores, visit online auctions, or even start attending races to find unique pieces for your collection.

From not even wanting to watch a race, to attending her first race my girlfriend has grown to like NASCAR and even has a little collection of her own by following those steps.

Why

My dad was a huge fan of NASCAR and is a major influence to why I love the motorsport today. He took me to my first ever race when I was a kid and I was instantly hooked. The first few pieces of memorabilia from my collection were hand-me-downs that came from my dad or my older brother.

I collect NASCAR memorabilia because it allows me to connect with the motorsport on a deeper level. Each item in my collection holds a special memory or represents a favorite driver or race. Some pieces in my collection represent important moments in NASCAR history so having a little bit of racing history is pretty cool.
